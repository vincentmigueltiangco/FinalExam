Gabaldon Province of Nueva Ecija

Gabaldon is a municipality in the landlocked province of Nueva Ecija.

The municipality has a land area of 225.28 square kilometers or 86.98 square miles which constitutes 3.96% of Nueva Ecija's total area. Its population as determined by the 2020 Census was 38,958. This represented 1.69% of the total population of Nueva Ecija province, or 0.31% of the overall population of the Central Luzon region. Based on these figures, the population density is computed at 173 inhabitants per square kilometer or 448 inhabitants per square mile.

Gabaldon Falls of Nueva Ecija

Amidst this bounty of nature, the province is also blessed with the fantastic scenery of Gabadon Falls. Named after the town of Gabaldon where it can be found, the ten-feet falls are surrounded by huge rock formations and rippling ice-cold water. It is located within the 200-hectare Sabani Estate Agricultural College. The hidden Gabaldon Falls has long been a favored destinations among tourists for its impressive scenery. Its cool and clear spring water comes from the Sierra Madre and has gotten recognition as the cleanest natural water source in the Philippines.

Nueva Ecija | a Cool dip at Gabaldon's Dupinga River

Dupinga River, formerly known as Sabani, lies at the foot of the Sierra Madre mountain ranges in the town of Gabaldon. It is one of the sources of livelihood of the locals there, who belong to the Dumagat indigenous group, as the local government lets them manage the tourism in the river and imparts fractions of its earnings to them. The local government also ensures the maintenance of cleanliness in the river, which is evident in the clearness of its waters. Its shallowness and the cold water from the Sierra Madre makes it an ideal place to escape the heat brought by the summer season.
